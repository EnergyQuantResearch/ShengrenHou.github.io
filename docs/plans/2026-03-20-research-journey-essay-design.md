# Research Journey Essay Design

**Goal:** Publish a second essay on the personal site based on a previously published WeChat interview article, while keeping the site’s essay system consistent, bilingual, and transparent about the original source.

## Product Decision

- The article will be published as an `Essay`, not as a separate media or interviews section.
- The text will be lightly edited rather than deeply rewritten.
- The article will retain the original conceptual structure, especially the `01 / 02 / 03` flow.
- The essay will explicitly note that it is adapted from a previously published media interview / WeChat article and will link to the original source.

## Content Direction

- Use a cleaner title suitable for the personal site while preserving the original spirit.
- Keep the three main sections:
  - imitation learning and decision-making
  - curiosity, realism, and innovation in research
  - the importance of advisors in doctoral growth
- Preserve the final reflective “life outside research” thread as a short closing section.

## UX Rules

- The essay should appear in homepage highlights and the essays index.
- The original source should be shown in a dedicated source block near the end of the essay page.
- The source block should be optional so the same layout can support future interview-based essays.

## Scope

This round includes:

- optional source attribution block in the essay layout
- one new bilingual essay adapted from the linked WeChat article
- homepage and essays index surfacing through the existing featured essay flow

This round does not include:

- a separate interviews/media section
- embedded media from the WeChat page itself
- importing the original page’s images or videos
